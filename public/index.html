About Techathon

Welcome to the Techathon event on April 22, 2023! This event is designed for participants who want to showcase their technical skills by developing innovative solutions to real-world problems. At the Techathon event, you have the flexibility to work on your project from the comfort of your own home. Unlike traditional Techathons, you don't have to develop your project on the event day. Instead, you can take your time to develop your project prior to the event and present it to a panel of judges on the event day.

Participants can choose to work individually or in teams of up to four members. Participants are encouraged to think creatively and come up with innovative solutions that address real-world problems. Projects can be developed in any programming language or technology stack.

To participate in the Techathon event, simply register on our website and submit your project by the designated deadline. At the end of the event, participants will have the opportunity to showcase their projects to a panel of judges and receive feedback on their solutions. The top projects will be awarded prizes and recognition.

We look forward to seeing the innovative solutions that participants develop during the Techathon event. Let's work together to make a positive impact in our communities and beyond.

Eligibility and Rules

  • The participant(s) should be students currently enrolled in BTech(any branch) or BCA course in Vaish College of Engineering.
  • The participant(s) can participate either as an individual or as a team with team size not exceeding 3 members.
  • Team formation guidelines:
    • The participants can be from any branch.
    • Cross-year teams are allowed.
    • One participant cannot be a member of more than one team.

  1. Register for the event:

    All participants must register for the Techathon event in advance. During the registration process, participants should provide their name, email address, and any other relevant information required by the event organizers.

  2. Form a team (if desired):

    Participants can form teams with other individuals or participate individually. If forming a team, participants should make sure to include individuals with complementary skills and interests.

  3. Develop the project:

    Participants can develop their projects from home in the weeks leading up to the event day. The project should solve a real-world problem.

  4. Submit the project:

    On the day of the Techathon event, participants will submit their projects to a panel of judges. Participants should make sure to have their projects completed and ready to present by the designated submission deadline.

  5. Present the project:

    After submitting the project, participants will present their projects to the panel of judges. The presentation should include a description of the problem the project solves, the process of developing the project, and the impact the project will have.

  6. Judging and prizes:

    The panel of judges will evaluate the projects based on their innovation, functionality, creativity, and impact. Winners will be announced and prizes will be awarded to the top projects.
